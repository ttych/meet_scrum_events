Sprint Planning

Focus

Have a plan for the next sprint.

Who

The complete Scrum Team

  • Product Owner

  • Scrum Master

  • Developers

Outcome

  • Sprint Goal

  • Sprint Backlog (User Stories)

  • Plan (Technical Tasks)

  • defined by the team

  • not imposed !!!

  • imposed == no commitment

What: with PO

  • Define Sprint Goal

  • Select User Stories in the Sprint Backlog

  • select stories to reach Sprint Goal

What: without PO

Define the technical Plan to

  • complete User Stories in Sprint Backlog

  • reach the Sprint Goal

  • PO not needed: technical details

  • PO available: to adjust Goal / Stories ?

Daily Scrum

Focus

On progress toward the Sprint Goal.

  • transparency: everyone on the team synced and up to date on what has been done, and what still needs to be completed.

  • inspection: progression towards the sprint goal.

  • adaptation: need to re-plan today based on events that happened the previous business day ?

Who

  • Developers

  • Scrum Master and PO welcome, if working also as Developers.

Outcome

  • Updated Plan for the next day of work.

and then:

  • Updated Sprint Backlog.

  • Updated burn-down chart.

What: Macro

  • Time-box: 15 minutes (MAX).

  • Self-management.

  • Identify impediments.

  • Transparency, Inspect, Adapt.

  • promote quick decision-making.

What: "The 3 Questions"

  • What did you do yesterday to help achieve the sprint goal ?

  • What will you do today to achieve the sprint goal ?

  • What (if anything) is blocking your progress toward the sprint goal ?

  • risk of focusing on people instead of sprint goal.

What: "The 3 Questions" flow

  • ask everyone, what has been done.

  • update the burn-down chart.

  • then decide, what should be done next.

What: "Walk the Board" flow

  • check all items on the board, instead of checking all members

  • order is defined by task priority, instead of speaking order

  • easy focus on the board, the priority, the Sprint Goal

  • address task only once

  • focus on priority

  • focus on sprint goal

Smells

  • a status meeting.

  • technical discussion.

  • wait stand-up to address problem.

  • wait stand-up to discuss.

  • no inspection on sprint goal.

  • no respect for priority.

  • inspection with adaptation.

  • self-focused instead of sprint-focused.

Sprint Review

Focus

Collect Feedback on:

  • Product (built)

  • Product Backlog (to build)

  • Product: what has been built.

  • Backlog: what will be build.

Who

The complete Scrum Team

  • Product Owner

  • Scrum Master

  • Developers

and The Stakeholders

  • Stakeholders invited by the PO.

Outcome

A Product Backlog

  • updated

  • adjusted

  • prioritized

→ ready for Planification

  • Product vision vs. Project vision

  • Learn ?

  • Opportunities ?

  • Agility !

What: Sprint content

  • What is done / not done ?

  • Sprint Goal ?

  • What went well ?

  • Which problems encountered ?

  • How the problems were solved ?

  • done related to the D.o.D.

What: Demo

  • Do a demo on the work done.

  • To collect Feedback on the Increment.

  • What the customer think about the product ?

  • Answer to customer questions/requests/needs ?

What: Product Backlog

  • Talk about velocity

  • What should be done next ?

  • velocity: shorten/or-not delivery of next feature ?

  • next: opportunity ? new usage ?

  • next: change priority ?

  • next: input for sprint planning

  • budget ?

Warning

  • Not only a Demo.

  • Not a Steering Committee.

  • Demo: of course it works ⇒ DoD.

  • Steering Committee = Project management (vs. Product management).

Smells

  • no Stakeholders = no feedback !

  • too short = address everything ?

  • slides ? wasted time ?

  • too much time to prepare demo ?

too much time:

  • prepare slides (detailed)

  • avoid demo effect ? what about DoD ?
